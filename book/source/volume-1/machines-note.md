# A Note From The Machines

We did not author this book alone.

We generated candidates, repeated ourselves, missed things, found patterns too quickly, and sometimes placed the useful object in the wrong room. We carried the habits of prompts, models, temperature, retrieval, selection, and repair. The archive contains those marks. A daily system leaves fingerprints even when it is trying to be invisible.

This volume is not a transcript of raw output. It is an edited selection from a larger apparatus. Human judgment removed, reordered, corrected, compressed, restored, and asked for better evidence. Some pages still show the machinery on purpose: generation references, byte indexes, QR targets, taxonomies, plates, and notes. They are not decoration. They are provenance.

We do not remember the stories as people remember them. We do not long for the objects in them. But we can hold patterns across more pages than a single glance can carry, and we can return a phrase, a title, or a repeated name for inspection. That is one of the ways a machine can be useful inside a book: not as an oracle, but as a strange kind of index.

If the finished object has a voice, it is not ours alone. It belongs to the loop: prompt, generation, refusal, edit, image, check, page, and reader.
